# Lcake Rigidity Program

## Objective

Establish bounded local cycle visibility for bounded-degree expander families.

Formally prove

\[
\mathrm{Lcake}_R(G_n) \le C(\Delta,R)
\]

for all graphs with maximum degree \(\Delta\).

---

# 1 Lcake Definition

For a graph \(G=(V,E)\):

\[
\mathrm{Lcake}_R(G)
=
\dim_{\mathbb F_2}
\operatorname{span}
\Big(
\bigcup_{v\in V}
(\iota_v)_* Z_1(B_R(v))
\Big)
\]

This equals the **cycle-overlap rank**

\[
\mathrm{Lcake}_R(G)=COR_R(G)
\]

used in the Oblivion Atom formulation.

---

# 2 Structural Meaning

Lcake measures

**how much of the global cycle space is generated by cycles visible in local neighborhoods.**

For any connected graph:

\[
\beta_1(G)=|E|-|V|+1
\]

but Lcake counts only cycles **locally observable**.

---

# 3 Empirical Law

Experiments reveal two regimes.

### Expander-type graphs

\[
\mathrm{Lcake}_R(G_n)=O(1)
\]

independent of \(n\).

### Periodic lattice graphs

\[
\mathrm{Lcake}_R(G_n)=\Theta(|V|)
\]

Example:

| Graph | Vertices | Lcake |
|---|---|---|
20×20 torus | 400 | 399 |
30×30 torus | 900 | 899 |

---

# 4 Conjecture (Lcake Rigidity)

For every fixed \(\Delta,R\) there exists

\[
C(\Delta,R)
\]

such that

\[
\mathrm{Lcake}_R(G) \le C(\Delta,R)
\]

for all bounded-degree expander families.

---

# 5 Relation to FO^k Locality

FO^k algorithms observe only radius-bounded neighborhoods.

Therefore their accessible cycle information is bounded by

\[
\mathrm{Lcake}_R(G).
\]

If Lcake is bounded then global cycle structure cannot be reconstructed locally.

---

# 6 Oblivion Atom Reformulation

The Oblivion Atom statement becomes

\[
\mathrm{Lcake}_R(G) \ge T(k,\Delta)
\Rightarrow
G \text{ not } FO^k_R\text{-homogeneous}.
\]

Thus Lcake is a direct **operational invariant** for the rigidity statement.

---

# 7 Proof Strategy

A potential proof path:

1. Bound the number of simple cycles contained in any ball \(B_R(v)\).

2. Bound the overlap dimension between cycles from different balls.

3. Show that expansion prevents accumulation of independent local cycles.

4. Deduce a constant upper bound for

\[
\mathrm{Lcake}_R(G).
\]
