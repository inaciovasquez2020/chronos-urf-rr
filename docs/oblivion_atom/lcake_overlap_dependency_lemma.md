# Lcake Overlap Dependency Lemma

## Lemma (Cycle Overlap Dependency)

Let \(G=(V,E)\) be a graph with maximum degree \(\Delta\).

Fix radius \(R\).

Let

\[
C_v = Z_1(B_R(v))
\]

denote the cycle space generated by cycles inside the radius-\(R\) ball around \(v\).

Then for vertices \(u,v\) with

\[
\mathrm{dist}(u,v) \le 2R
\]

the corresponding cycle spaces satisfy

\[
C_u \cap C_v \neq \{0\}.
\]

In particular the cycles generated by \(B_R(u)\) and \(B_R(v)\) are not independent.

---

## Consequence

Let

\[
\mathcal{C} = \bigcup_{v\in V} C_v
\]

be the set of all locally visible cycles.

Because radius-\(R\) balls overlap heavily, the cycles produced by different balls satisfy many linear dependencies.

Thus

\[
\dim
\operatorname{span}(\mathcal{C})
\ll
\sum_v \dim C_v .
\]

---

## Implication for Lcake

Recall

\[
\mathrm{Lcake}_R(G)
=
\dim
\operatorname{span}
\Big(
\bigcup_v Z_1(B_R(v))
\Big).
\]

The overlap lemma implies that local cycle spaces cannot accumulate linearly across the graph.

Instead they collapse into a much smaller global span.

---

## Relation to Expansion

In expander graphs

* balls grow rapidly
* local neighborhoods overlap strongly
* cycles generated in different balls share large edge support

This produces strong global linear dependencies.

---

## Program Role

The Oblivion Atom program requires showing that the number of independent cycles observable locally remains bounded.

The overlap dependency lemma provides the structural mechanism forcing this collapse.
