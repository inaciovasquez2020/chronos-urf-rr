\title{Short-Cycle Propagation Lemma}
\date{}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\section*{Statement}

Let \(G\) be a graph with maximum degree \(\Delta\).

Let \(C_1,C_2\) be cycles whose vertex signatures agree under
\(\mathrm{FO}^k\) radius-\(R\) neighborhood types.

Let

\[
D = C_1 \oplus C_2 .
\]

\begin{lemma}[Propagation Bound]

If edges agree on every segment of length \(k\) along the cycles,
then every connected component of \(D\) lies inside a tube of
radius \(r=\min(R/2,3^k)\) around some vertex of the cycles.

\end{lemma}

\section*{Purpose}

This lemma reduces the Short-Cycle Symmetric-Difference Lemma to a
local tube containment statement.
