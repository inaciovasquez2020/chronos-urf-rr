\section{Cycle--Overlap Visibility Conjecture}

Let \(G\) be a graph with maximum degree \(\Delta\).
Fix radius \(R\).

\subsection{Empirical Observation}

Experiments on bounded-degree expanders indicate that
large cycle-overlap rank produces vertex diversity
detectable by the Weisfeiler--Leman refinement.

Measured scaling:

\[
COR_R(G) \uparrow
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
|\chi_{WL^2}(G)| \uparrow .
\]

\subsection{Conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[Cycle--Overlap Visibility]
There exist constants \(c>0\) and \(\alpha>0\) depending only on
\(\Delta\) and \(R\) such that for every graph \(G\)

\[
|\chi_{WL^2}(G)|
\;\ge\;
c \cdot COR_R(G)^{\alpha}.
\]
\end{conjecture}

\subsection{Implication}

If the conjecture holds then

\[
COR_R(G)
\Rightarrow
WL^2\text{ diversity}
\Rightarrow
FO^3\text{ type diversity}.
\]

\subsection{Experimental Evidence}

Experiments on random \(4\)-regular graphs with \(n=12000\)
and radius \(R=6\) produced the following WL\(^2\) scaling:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{c|c}
sample size & WL\(^2\) colors \\
\hline
50 & 2 \\
100 & 2 \\
200 & 2 \\
400 & 4 \\
600 & 10 \\
800 & 15
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

These observations support the hypothesis that cycle-overlap
rank acts as a structural source of WL\(^2\) distinguishability.
