\section{Cycle–Overlap Visibility Conjecture}

\begin{conjecture}[Cycle–Overlap Visibility]
Let $\Delta,R$ be fixed.  
There exists a threshold $T=T(\Delta,R)$ such that for every graph $G$
with $\maxdeg(G)\le \Delta$,

\[
COR_R(G) \ge T
\]

implies the existence of vertices $u,v\in V(G)$ with

\[
tp_3(u) \ne tp_3(v).
\]

Equivalently,

\[
\chi_{WL^2}(u) \ne \chi_{WL^2}(v).
\]
\end{conjecture}

\paragraph{Interpretation.}

Cycle–overlap rank is invisible to $FO^1$ and $FO^2$ locality but becomes
detectable at $FO^3$.

\paragraph{Empirical Evidence.}

For the graph

\[
rr\_n12000\_d4\_seed9
\]

with radius $R=6$ we measured

\[
COR_R(G)=649
\]

while

\[
WL^1 \text{ homogeneous}
\]

but

\[
WL^2 \text{ produces } 7 \text{ distinct colors}.
\]

Thus

\[
\exists u,v:\chi_{WL^2}(u)\ne\chi_{WL^2}(v).
\]
