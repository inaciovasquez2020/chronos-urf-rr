# Newstein Rooted-Ball Trivialization Lemma

## Status
OPEN

## Statement
Let \(G\) be a bounded-degree graph, let \(B_R(v)\) denote the rooted radius-\(R\) ball at \(v\), and let
\[
\pi_R : Z^1(B_R(v)) \to Q_R(v)
\]
be the Newstein local quotient map to the relevant rooted quotient object \(Q_R(v)\).

Assume the Newstein Local Coboundary Criterion holds on \(B_R(v)\).
Then every class in \(Q_R(v)\) represented by a rooted-local cycle-supported cocycle is trivial.

Equivalently,
\[
Q_R(v)=0.
\]

## Role
This is the weakest sufficient local theorem needed to collapse the rooted quotient term in the Newstein chain.

## Dependency
Depends only on:
1. Newstein Local Coboundary Criterion.
2. Definitions of rooted ball, local cocycle, and quotient object.

## Consequence
If this lemma holds uniformly on all rooted radius-\(R\) balls, then the local obstruction term in the Newstein quotient-gap reduction vanishes.
