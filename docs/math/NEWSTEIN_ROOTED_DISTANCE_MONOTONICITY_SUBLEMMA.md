# Newstein Rooted-Distance Monotonicity Sublemma

## Status
OPEN

## Statement
Let \(T\subseteq B_R(v)\) be a rooted spanning tree of the rooted-local patch.
Let \(w\) be a non-root vertex in its tree component, and let
\[
p=\operatorname{par}_T(w)
\]
be its parent in \(T\).

Then rooted distance is nonincreasing under one-step parent iteration:
\[
d(v,p)\le d(v,w).
\]

## Role
This is the weakest sufficient missing sublemma for the Newstein One-Step Parent Stability Predicate.

## Consequence
Once proved, one-step parent stability follows immediately.
