# Newstein Fiber-to-Global Injection Lemma

## Status
OPEN

## Statement
Let
\[
\Phi : \bigoplus_{i \in I} Q(F_i) \to Q_{\mathrm{global}}
\]
be the canonical map from the direct sum of residual fiber quotients to the global Newstein quotient.

Then \(\Phi\) is injective.

Equivalently,
\[
\ker(\Phi)=0.
\]

## Role
This prevents hidden cancellation across fibers.

## Consequence
Once the fiber rank is known, injectivity transfers the fiber lower bound to the global quotient.
