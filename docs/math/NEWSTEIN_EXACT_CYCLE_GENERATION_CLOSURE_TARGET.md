# Newstein Exact Cycle-Generation Closure Target

Status: PROVED

## Statement

Let \(L\) be the rooted local complex and let \(T \subseteq L\) be a spanning tree. For every non-tree edge \(e \in E(L)\setminus E(T)\), let \(P_T(e)\) denote the unique tree path in \(T\) joining the endpoints of \(e\), and define the fundamental cycle
\[
C_e := e + P_T(e).
\]

Then:

1. \(C_e\) is rooted-local.
2. \(C_e\) lies in the rooted-local generating family.
3. The family \(\{C_e : e\in E(L)\setminus E(T)\}\) spans \(Z_1(L)\).

## Proof

### 1. Rooted-locality

Fix a root in the rooted local complex \(L\). Because \(T\) is a spanning tree of \(L\), every vertex of \(L\) lies in the rooted local complex and every edge of \(T\) is rooted-local. For a non-tree edge \(e=uv\), the path \(P_T(e)\) is the unique \(u\)-to-\(v\) path in \(T\). Hence every edge of \(P_T(e)\) lies in \(L\). Therefore every edge of
\[
C_e = e + P_T(e)
\]
lies in \(L\), so \(C_e\) is rooted-local.

### 2. Membership in the rooted-local generating family

By the rooted-local triangle scheme, every cycle supported in the rooted local complex reduces to a sum of rooted-local triangle cycles. The cycle \(C_e\) is rooted-local by Step 1, hence it belongs to the rooted-local cycle space generated by the rooted-local triangle scheme. Therefore \(C_e\) lies in the rooted-local generating family.

### 3. Spanning of \(Z_1(L)\)

Let \(z \in Z_1(L)\). Over \(\mathbf F_2\), write
\[
z = \sum_{a \in \operatorname{supp}(z)\setminus E(T)} a \;+\; \sum_{b \in \operatorname{supp}(z)\cap E(T)} b.
\]
For each non-tree edge \(a\), add the fundamental cycle \(C_a = a + P_T(a)\). Then
\[
z + \sum_{a \in \operatorname{supp}(z)\setminus E(T)} C_a
\]
contains no non-tree edges, because each \(a\) appears exactly once from \(z\) and exactly once from \(C_a\), so these cancel in \(\mathbf F_2\). The remaining chain is supported entirely on \(T\). Since \(T\) is a tree, it has no nonzero 1-cycles. Hence
\[
z + \sum_{a \in \operatorname{supp}(z)\setminus E(T)} C_a = 0,
\]
so
\[
z = \sum_{a \in \operatorname{supp}(z)\setminus E(T)} C_a.
\]
Thus the family \(\{C_e : e\in E(L)\setminus E(T)\}\) spans \(Z_1(L)\).
