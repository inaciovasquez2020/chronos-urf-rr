# Newstein Sigma Growth vs Local-Type Capacity

Status: OPEN

## Statement

Assume the Non-Factorization from Sigma Growth package:
\[
\dim_{\mathbf F_2}(Z_1/W^{\mathrm{glob}})\to\infty
\]
along the chosen graph family.

Assume a bounded local-type factorization model, namely that the quotient invariant \(Q\) factors through a finite local state space depending only on \((r,k,\Delta)\).

Then the image dimension of the induced quotient is uniformly bounded:
\[
\dim_{\mathbf F_2}(Z_1/W^{\mathrm{glob}})\le C(r,k,\Delta),
\]
for some constant \(C(r,k,\Delta)\).

Therefore the two statements are incompatible, and bounded local-type factorization fails.

## Role

This is the exact contradiction surface between unbounded sigma-growth and bounded local-type capacity.
