# Newstein Injectivity-to-Global Direct-Sum Theorem

Status: OPEN

## Statement

Assume the Diameter-Separation to Injectivity Theorem.

Let \(U\) be a family of pairwise distinct fibers, and for each \(u\in U\) let
\[
V_u\subseteq Z_1(B_r(u);\mathbf F_2)/W_u
\]
be a nonzero \(2\)-dimensional twisted subspace furnished by the sigma-package.

Then the induced map
\[
\bigoplus_{u\in U} V_u \longrightarrow Z_1/W^{\mathrm{glob}}
\]
is injective.

Equivalently, the global quotient contains the direct sum of the local sigma-fiber twisted subspaces.

## Role

This is the exact theorem-level step upgrading cross-fiber injectivity into a genuine global direct-sum embedding.
