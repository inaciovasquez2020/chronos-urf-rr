# Newstein Diameter-Separation Filling Obstruction

Status: OPEN

## Statement

Fix distinct vertices \(u\neq v\). Let
\[
[c_u]\in Z_1(B_r(u);\mathbf F_2)/W_u,
\qquad
[c_v]\in Z_1(B_r(v);\mathbf F_2)/W_v
\]
be nonzero local twisted classes.

Then every \(2\)-chain \(S\) satisfying
\[
\partial S=c_u-c_v
\]
must satisfy
\[
\operatorname{diam}(\operatorname{supp} S)>L.
\]

Equivalently, no \(L\)-bounded filling can identify nonzero twisted classes from distinct fibers.

## Role

This is the weakest theorem-level obstruction still needed to turn the sigma-package into a genuine global direct-sum embedding.
