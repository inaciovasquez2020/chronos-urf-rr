# FO^k Simple Cycle Definability Obstruction

FO^k cannot define simple cycles of length greater than k.

Reason:
- FO^k has only k variables.
- Enforcing pairwise distinctness of ℓ vertices requires ℓ variables.

Therefore Step 2 of the original CLR proof cannot hold in FO^k.

Resolution strategies:
1. Replace FO^k with C^k (counting logic)
2. Use k-dimensional Weisfeiler–Leman equivalence
3. Use homological cycle-space invariants
