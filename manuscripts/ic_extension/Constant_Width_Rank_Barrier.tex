\section{Constant-Width Rank Barrier for Per-Constraint Communication Arguments}

\subsection{Setup}

Let $R \subseteq \{0,1\}^w$ be a Boolean relation of arity $w$.
Fix a partition of its variables into disjoint sets $X$ and $Y$
with $|X|=a$, $|Y|=b$, and $a+b=w$.

Define the violated-constraint predicate:
\[
f_R(x,y) = \mathbf{1}[(x,y)\notin R].
\]

Let $M_{f_R} \in \mathbb{F}_2^{2^a \times 2^b}$
denote the communication matrix over $\mathbb{F}_2$.

\subsection{Main Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Constant-Width Rank Barrier]
For every Boolean relation $R$ of arity $w$ and every partition $(X,Y)$,
\[
\rank_{\mathbb{F}_2}(M_{f_R}) \le 2^{\min(a,b)} \le 2^{w/2}.
\]
In particular, if $w=O(1)$ then
\[
\rank_{\mathbb{F}_2}(M_{f_R}) = O(1).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The matrix $M_{f_R}$ has at most $2^a$ rows and $2^b$ columns.
Hence
\[
\rank(M_{f_R}) \le \min(2^a,2^b)=2^{\min(a,b)}.
\]
Since $\min(a,b)\le w/2$, the result follows.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Tightness}

The bound is tight: the inner product relation
\[
\mathrm{IP}_{w/2}(x,y)=\bigoplus_{i=1}^{w/2} x_i y_i
\]
achieves rank $2^{w/2}$.

Thus non-separable constant-width relations exist,
but their rank remains $O(1)$.

\subsection{Corollary (Per-Constraint Rank Limitation)}

For any constraint system consisting of $m=O(n)$
constant-width constraints:

\[
\max_{C} \rank_{\mathbb{F}_2}(M_{f_C}) = O(1).
\]

Therefore no per-constraint rank argument
can yield $\Omega(n)$ communication lower bounds.

Exponential lower bounds must arise from
\emph{global structure} (e.g., expansion and resolution width),
not clause-local rank amplification.

\subsection{Conclusion}

The per-constraint bilinear amplification program
is structurally impossible for constant-width systems.
The rank barrier is absolute.
