\section{Linear-Ideal Collapse and Characteristic Matching}

\subsection{Linear-Ideal Collapse Theorem}

Let $F = \{p_1,\dots,p_m\} \subset \mathbb F[z_1,\dots,z_n]$ with Boolean axioms
$\{z_i^2 - z_i : i \in [n]\}$.
Let $I = \langle F \cup \{z_i^2 - z_i\}\rangle$.

\paragraph{Theorem.}
\[
\deg_{\mathbb F}(F \vdash \bot) = 1
\]
if and only if
\[
1 \in I \cap \mathbb F[z]_{\le 1}.
\]

Equivalently, the contradiction appears in the first graded component
of the ideal.

\subsection{Characteristic-Matching Principle}

If all constraints reduce to linear forms over $\mathbb F$ modulo
$z_i^2 - z_i$ and the induced linear system is inconsistent over $\mathbb F$,
then there exists a degree-1 Polynomial Calculus refutation.

Hardness arises only when $1$ first appears in higher graded
components of the ideal; expansion can then force
\[
\deg_{\mathbb F}(F \vdash \bot) = \Omega(n).
\]

\subsection{Canonical Example: Tseitin}

Over $\mathbb F_2$: constraints are linear, and $1$ lies in the degree-1 span.

Over $\operatorname{char}(\mathbb F)\neq 2$: constraints have degree $d$,
no linear witness exists, and expansion yields $\Omega(n)$ degree.
