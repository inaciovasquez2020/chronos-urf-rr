\section{CDCL/Clause-Learning to Resolution Bridge}

\subsection{Learning Trace Model}
A CDCL-style solver with clause learning produces a sequence of learned clauses
$C_1,C_2,\dots,C_T$ such that each $C_t$ is implied by $F$.

\subsection{Resolution Simulation}
A learned clause derived by conflict analysis with standard schemes
(1UIP and related) is derivable by resolution from $F$ together with prior learned clauses.

\subsection{Bridge Statement (Target)}
For any CDCL run producing learned clauses $C_1,\dots,C_T$ and refuting $F$,
there exists a resolution refutation whose derived clauses include
a subset simulating the learned clauses, with width bounded by
\[
\max\{ \max_t \mathrm{width}(C_t),\ w_0(F)\}.
\]
Thus any lower bound on $W(F)$ implies that any CDCL refutation must learn
some clause of width at least $W(F)$, and hence must incur corresponding resource cost.
