\section*{Statement}

Let $\Phi$ be a functional on clause multisets satisfying:

\begin{itemize}
\item[(P1)] permutation invariance,
\item[(P2)] $\Phi(M \cup \{C\}) \le \Phi(M) + c$ for a universal constant $c$,
\item[(P3)] monotonicity under derivation.
\end{itemize}

\begin{theorem}
Such a $\Phi$ cannot distinguish Tseitin formulas
requiring exponential resolution
from polynomially refutable formulas,
in the sense that it cannot satisfy
$\Phi(T_{\text{hard}})=\Omega(n)$ and
$\Phi(T_{\text{easy}})=o(n)$ simultaneously.
\end{theorem}

\noindent
The obstruction arises because
resolution hardness in Tseitin formulas
is driven by exponential multiplicity
over identical variable supports,
which any support-symmetric functional
compresses.
